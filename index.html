Introduction

Martin Luther King Junior, born on January 15, 1929, Atlanta, Georgia, U.S - died April 4, 1968, Memphis, Tennessee was a baptist minister, social activist who led the civil rights movement in the US and the youngest man to win the nobel peace prize. His leadership was fundamental to that movement's success in ending the leagal segregration of African Americans in the South and other parts of the US. He was awarded the Nobel Peace Prize in 1964 at the age of 35.
